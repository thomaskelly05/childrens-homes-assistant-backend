This document defines decision ownership. Per founder decision, **roles are defined
separately even where one person currently holds several.** Naming the roles separately is
how the concentration of responsibility is made visible and, in time, distributed.

---

## 1. Why separate roles when one person holds them

Phase 1 found that all governance traces to a single founder, with **no named Safeguarding
Lead or Data Protection Officer** (`open-questions.md` Q3; Review Board, Investor lens). If
roles are not named, gaps cannot be seen and accountability cannot move. This document names
each role and its accountability **regardless of current holder**, so that:

- the unfilled binding-charter roles (Safeguarding Lead, DPO) are visible as gaps, not
  hidden inside "the founder does everything";
- concentration of roles in one person is recorded as a bus-factor risk;
- future appointments have a defined seat to fill.

## 4. Decision rights and escalation

- Each ratified document names an **Owner** accountable for it (see metadata headers).
- A lower structural tier (00 §2a) does not acquire decision rights over a higher tier.
- Binding charters (O4 Safeguarding, O5 Privacy) carry override authority within their
  domain regardless of who holds the seat (00 §2c).
- Where a decision is contested, the conflict-resolution procedure (00 §4) applies, escalating
  ultimately to the Founder, with the decision and its evidence recorded.

---

## 5. Conflict-of-interest and concentration risk (honest record)

**INFERRED risk.** One person currently holds Founder, Product, Engineering, AI Safety and
Documentation ownership, and interim Release/Operations and Commercial responsibility. This
means, today, AI-safety and release decisions can be made and signed off by the same person
who sets product and commercial direction. This is a recognised **bus-factor and
independence risk** (`open-questions.md` Q3; Review Board). It is recorded, not resolved.

The two roles that most need independence — **Safeguarding Lead** and **Data Protection
Officer** — are precisely the two that are unfilled. Until appointed, their binding charters
(O4, O5) are held on an interim basis by the Founder, which the constitution names as a gap
rather than a satisfactory state.
