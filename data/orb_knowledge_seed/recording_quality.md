# Recording Quality Guidance

A good daily note should be factual, child-centred, include the child's voice where possible, and describe adult responses without judgemental language.

Avoid labels such as attention seeking, manipulative or bad behaviour. Record what happened, what the child may have been communicating, how staff responded, and what changed afterwards.

For incidents, include sequence, immediate safety actions, notifications, and follow-up. Evidence should show difference made, not only that a form was completed.
