Incident record

Record when the incident happened or when it became known.
Use a clear incident type that helps with reporting and review.
Record where the incident happened.
Record the level of concern or impact.
Describe what happened clearly and factually without blame or opinion.
Record any known trigger, build-up, or context before the incident.
Describe presentation, affect, regulation, and behaviour in a respectful way.
Record what adults did, including de-escalation, boundaries, and support offered.
Record how the young person responded to support, direction, or intervention.
Include the young person’s voice or meaning where known.
Record how the incident ended and what happened immediately afterwards.
Record notifications, repairs, reviews, body maps, tasks, and next steps.
Record any restorative conversation, repair, or relationship support.

Recording guidance

Good incident records should be factual, respectful, and specific. They should explain: what happened, context, adult support, the young person's response, outcome, and follow-up accountability.
