Education record

Record the date the education update relates to.
Choose the attendance outcome for the day or session.
Record the education setting or provision involved.
Record concentration, participation, effort, emotional readiness to learn, and engagement across the session or day.
Record behaviour, regulation, relationships, anxieties, dysregulation, or positive changes linked to education.
Record achievements, positive participation, effort, progress, attendance improvement, or praise received.
Record concerns such as non-attendance, behaviour issues, emotional distress, conflict, exclusions, or communication from school.
Record what action was taken by the home, school, or other professionals, including support, follow-up, phone calls, meetings, or practical action.
Record the professional spoken to or involved where known.
If follow-up is required, record the next relevant date.
Record the young person’s views, worries, wishes, motivation, feelings about learning, or any direct comments about school or college.
Record anything that needs to be handed over, monitored, reviewed, escalated, or followed up by staff or managers.
