# IndiCare Premium Identity & Connect Design Language

Status: Active
Date: 2026-05-17
Audience:
- frontend engineering
- product design
- UX
- platform architecture

---

# Purpose

This document defines the premium visual and emotional design language for:

- adult identity;
- child identity;
- dashboard personalisation;
- IndiCare Connect;
- notifications;
- handover;
- operational workspace experiences.

The purpose is to ensure IndiCare evolves into:

- a calm operational environment;
- a personalised workspace;
- an emotionally intelligent platform;
- a recognisable premium product.

---

# Core design philosophy

IndiCare should feel like:

> Apple designed operational software for residential children’s homes.

The platform should feel:

- warm;
- premium;
- personal;
- human;
- emotionally intelligent;
- operationally calm.

NOT:

- corporate;
- clinical;
- dashboard-heavy;
- admin-heavy;
- visually exhausting.

---

# Emotional design goals

Users should feel:

- recognised;
- organised;
- calm;
- supported;
- operationally aware.

Children should feel:

- represented;
- humanised;
- understood.

Homes should feel:

- alive;
- connected;
- operationally coherent.

---

# Visual hierarchy

## The platform

The platform itself should remain:

- calm;
- soft;
- spacious;
- breathable.

Use:

- warm white backgrounds;
- soft neutral surfaces;
- minimal borders;
- subtle shadows only where necessary;
- generous spacing;
- large readable typography.

---

## ORB

ORB is intentionally different.

ORB should remain:

- vibrant;
- luminous;
- colourful;
- hue-rich;
- energetic;
- visually alive.

ORB is the emotional/intelligence layer.

The platform is the operational calm layer.

This contrast is intentional.

---

# IndiCare colour philosophy

## Primary colour

IndiCare Blue.

Use vibrant electric blue for:

- primary actions;
- active states;
- highlights;
- operational focus;
- notifications;
- selected states.

Avoid overusing strong colour.

The UI should remain calm overall.

---

## Accent gradients

Use subtle luminous gradients for:

- welcome cards;
- Connect highlights;
- profile headers;
- ORB transitions;
- key operational focus moments.

Gradients should feel:

- premium;
- subtle;
- modern.

Never overwhelming.

---

# Typography

Typography is a major part of the premium feel.

Use:

- large section headings;
- strong hierarchy;
- readable chronology spacing;
- minimal dense text blocks.

Avoid:

- tiny metadata-heavy text;
- compressed operational tables;
- visual overload.

---

# Adult identity design

Adult profile pages should feel:

- personal;
- professional;
- warm;
- useful.

The system should clearly know:

- who is logged in;
- what matters to them;
- their operational focus;
- their home;
- their children.

Adult profiles should include:

- profile image;
- preferred name;
- role;
- home;
- quick actions;
- recent activity;
- pinned workflows;
- operational priorities;
- favourite children;
- Connect activity.

---

# Child identity design

Children must NEVER feel like:

- cases;
- records;
- risk objects.

Child profiles should feel:

- human;
- warm;
- person-first.

Above the fold should prioritise:

- image;
- preferred name;
- what matters to me;
- what helps me;
- communication style;
- sensory needs;
- strengths/interests;
- important relationships.

Risk and safeguarding remain visible,
BUT:
should not dominate the emotional feel of the page.

---

# Dashboard philosophy

The dashboard should not feel like:

- enterprise analytics;
- KPI reporting;
- admin overload.

The dashboard should feel like:

> today’s operational awareness.

The dashboard should answer:

- what changed?
- who needs attention?
- what is unresolved?
- what matters to me today?

---

# Personalisation philosophy

Adults should be able to:

- pin widgets;
- reorder widgets;
- favourite children;
- favourite templates;
- save layout preferences.

BUT:

critical safeguarding and missing alerts cannot disappear.

Personalisation should remain:

- structured;
- calm;
- safe.

Avoid:

- drag-and-drop chaos;
- dashboard sprawl;
- clutter-heavy layouts.

---

# IndiCare Connect philosophy

IndiCare Connect should feel:

- modern;
- calm;
- familiar;
- conversational;
- professional.

Think:

- Apple Messages;
- Teams;
- Slack;
- WhatsApp.

BUT:

- safeguarding-aware;
- provider-scoped;
- audit-aware;
- operationally bounded.

---

# Connect UI principles

Connect should support:

- rapid communication;
- continuity between shifts;
- safe operational updates;
- child-linked discussion where appropriate.

Avoid:

- clutter-heavy sidebars;
- enterprise messaging density;
- confusing thread structures.

---

# Notification philosophy

Notifications should:

- reduce uncertainty;
- reduce missed follow-up;
- improve operational continuity.

Notifications should NOT:

- overwhelm;
- spam;
- create alert fatigue.

Use:

- calm unread badges;
- prioritised alerts;
- subtle motion;
- grouped notifications.

---

# Welcome experience philosophy

The welcome experience should feel:

- personal;
- grounding;
- operationally useful.

Example:

“Good morning, Theo. Here’s what matters at Rosewood House today.”

The welcome should:

- orient the adult;
- reduce operational uncertainty;
- surface important follow-up;
- support shift continuity.

---

# Empty state philosophy

Empty states must remain:

- honest;
- calm;
- useful.

Never fabricate operational context.

Avoid:

- fake counts;
- fake profiles;
- fake messages;
- fake chronology.

---

# Mobile philosophy

IndiCare should feel premium on:

- phones;
- tablets;
- laptops;
- desktops.

Mobile layouts should:

- remain readable;
- remain spacious;
- prioritise important operational context;
- support quick actions;
- support Connect naturally.

---

# Final principle

IndiCare should increasingly feel like:

- a living operational environment;
- a connected home workspace;
- an emotionally intelligent operational platform.

NOT:

- compliance software;
- admin tooling;
- dashboard-heavy enterprise software.
