# IndiCare premium operational design system

## Product intent

IndiCare should feel like calm operational clarity for residential care staff: child-centred, inspection-ready and useful within two minutes of login. It should not feel like generic admin software, dashboard clutter or AI theatre.

## Principles

1. Clarity over complexity.
2. Calm over noise.
3. One source of truth.
4. No duplicate cards.
5. No dashboard clutter.
6. Fewer boxes, stronger hierarchy.
7. Child-first, evidence-backed.
8. Staff should understand the child or home within two minutes.
9. Assistant supports, but never dominates.
10. Premium simplicity, not corporate complexity.
11. British English throughout.
12. Accessible for staff of all digital abilities.

## Visual direction

- Background: warm white and soft neutral surfaces.
- Text: deep charcoal for primary reading.
- Accent: calm blue/indigo for primary actions and focus.
- Amber: attention, review, evidence missing.
- Green: resolved, healthy, complete.
- Red: urgent safeguarding or immediate risk only.
- Borders: fewer and softer; prefer whitespace and grouping.
- Typography: larger, readable, confident.
- Cards: soft cards only where they clarify grouping.
- Icons: functional only; no decorative clutter.
- Motion: minimal, respectful of reduced motion.
- ORB: premium, calm, interruptible, explicitly review-led.

## Page anatomy

Every major page should contain:

1. Simple page title.
2. One-line human summary.
3. Primary action.
4. What needs attention.
5. Main content.
6. Supporting evidence/details.
7. Clear next steps.

## Canonical surfaces

- Command Centre owns the cross-home attention queue.
- Young Person overview owns the two-minute child picture.
- Child chronology owns source-linked events.
- Documents owns templates, upload, review, sign-off and search.
- Evidence owns inspection-ready traceability.
- Safeguarding owns open safeguarding concerns and follow-up.
- Inspection readiness owns gaps, evidence and action plan.
- Governance owns provider controls, assistant safety and audit.
- Assistant/ORB owns drafting, reflection and retrieval support only.

## Interaction rules

- One concept appears once; contextual pages link back to it.
- Primary actions use direct verbs: "Add daily note", "Open chronology", "Upload supporting evidence".
- Empty states explain what is missing and what to do next.
- Loading states stay calm and never imply failure.
- Error states are specific and non-blaming.
- Critical actions are visible, keyboard reachable and at least 44px high.
- Search results show title, type, date, linked child/home, why it matters and quick open.

## Copy rules

- Use calm British English.
- Avoid punitive labels, clinical certainty and AI-sounding claims.
- Prefer "This may need follow-up" to "Non-compliant".
- Prefer "Needs manager review" to "Failed".
- Say "not yet configured" where backend support is missing.
- Never let demo or fallback data appear as live care information.
