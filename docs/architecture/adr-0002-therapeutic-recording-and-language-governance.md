# ADR-0002 — Therapeutic recording and language governance

Status: Accepted
Date: 2026-05-17
Decision makers: CTO / product architecture / therapeutic recording direction

---

# Context

IndiCare OS is intended to become a therapeutically informed operational platform for residential children’s homes.

Historically, many care-recording systems evolved from:

- compliance-first workflows;
- punitive behaviour language;
- incident-heavy operational culture;
- inspection-defensive writing;
- fragmented relational understanding.

This often produces records that:

- dehumanise children;
- over-focus on behaviour instead of need;
- reinforce negative narratives;
- weaken reflective practice;
- create emotionally unsafe recording culture;
- reduce operational clarity.

IndiCare is intentionally taking a different direction.

The platform already includes:

- therapeutic document templates;
- child voice structures;
- chronology projection;
- operational memory;
- safeguarding direction;
- evidence mapping;
- provider oversight;
- assistant operational guidance.

A formal platform-level decision is therefore required.

---

# Decision

IndiCare adopts therapeutic, trauma-informed and relationally aware recording governance as a core product principle.

This means:

- operational records should support understanding, not merely compliance;
- templates should encourage reflection, not punishment;
- child voice should be structurally supported;
- language guidance should avoid dehumanising terminology;
- operational systems should support repair and restorative thinking;
- safeguarding and operational clarity must remain strong.

This does NOT mean:

- minimising safeguarding concerns;
- avoiding factual recording;
- removing accountability;
- hiding operational risk.

The goal is:

- emotionally intelligent operational recording.

---

# Consequences

## Positive consequences

### 1. Safer recording culture

The platform encourages:

- reflective practice;
- relational understanding;
- child-centred recording;
- professional curiosity;
- restorative thinking.

This may reduce:

- punitive narrative drift;
- emotionally reactive language;
- escalation-oriented recording culture.

---

### 2. Stronger child understanding

Records increasingly support:

- emotional presentation;
- communication needs;
- sensory context;
- known triggers;
- what helped;
- what did not help;
- repair attempts;
- child voice.

This improves continuity of care.

---

### 3. Better operational context

Operational leaders can increasingly understand:

- patterns;
- unmet need;
- relational strain;
- safeguarding pressure;
- placement stability factors.

Rather than reviewing isolated incidents.

---

### 4. Improved inspection defensibility

Therapeutic recording can strengthen evidence for:

- positive relationships;
- child-centred care;
- reflective practice;
- safeguarding understanding;
- leadership oversight.

---

# Platform requirements

## Templates must be structurally distinct

Templates should reflect:

- operational purpose;
- emotional context;
- safeguarding significance;
- evidential role;
- leadership responsibility.

A:

- daily note,
- safeguarding concern,
- key work session,
- missing episode,
- Reg 44 evidence note,
- behaviour support reflection

should not share identical structure.

---

## Child voice should be structurally supported

Where relevant, templates should support:

- what the child said;
- what the child felt;
- what the child wanted;
- what the child found difficult;
- what the child felt helped.

Child voice should not be an afterthought field.

---

## Behaviour should be contextualised

Templates should encourage understanding of:

- distress;
- communication;
- trauma triggers;
- sensory overload;
- unmet need;
- relational context;
- environmental stressors.

This does not replace factual recording.

---

## Restorative and repair work should be visible

Where relevant, workflows should support:

- repair conversations;
- debriefs;
- reflection;
- restorative actions;
- follow-up planning.

---

# Language governance

## Avoid language such as

- attention seeking
- manipulative
- kicked off
- bad behaviour
- non-compliant
- naughty
- playing up
- refusing for the sake of it

unless directly quoting and operationally necessary.

---

## Prefer language such as

- appeared distressed
- appeared dysregulated
- communicated through behaviour
- support offered
- unable to engage at that time
- what may have contributed
- what helped
- what did not help
- emotional presentation
- repair/restorative work
- child’s perspective

---

# Assistant/ORB implications

Assistant systems should:

- avoid punitive summarisation;
- avoid emotionally inflammatory language;
- avoid certainty beyond evidence;
- encourage reflective practice;
- surface chronology and evidence context.

Assistant systems must NOT:

- make diagnostic claims;
- label children;
- produce deterministic risk labels;
- replace safeguarding judgement.

---

# Regulatory alignment

This direction supports operational evidence relevant to:

- Views, Wishes and Feelings Standard;
- Positive Relationships Standard;
- Protection of Children Standard;
- Leadership and Management Standard;
- Care Planning Standard.

It also supports SCCIF themes including:

- experiences and progress of children;
- help and protection;
- leadership effectiveness.

This is operational guidance, not legal advice.

---

# Rejected alternatives

## Alternative: neutral generic compliance recording

Rejected because:

- weak relational understanding;
- poor emotional continuity;
- punitive drift risk;
- limited therapeutic value.

---

## Alternative: AI-generated “wellbeing scoring”

Rejected because:

- weak explainability;
- safeguarding risk;
- pseudo-clinical authority concerns;
- poor operational trust.

---

## Alternative: heavily sanitised recording

Rejected because:

- safeguarding clarity still matters;
- operational facts must remain visible;
- leaders require accurate chronology.

Therapeutic recording is not avoidance of difficult operational truth.

---

# Implementation guidance

Future workflows/templates should:

1. support child voice;
2. support reflective practice;
3. support chronology linkage;
4. support evidence linkage;
5. encourage emotionally intelligent language;
6. remain operationally factual;
7. support safeguarding defensibility.
